Short Biography:

This is Malek Shafi’I. I'm director and funder of Afghanistan International Human Rights Film Festival. I am also filmmaker, director, producer, editor, consultant. I studied sociology in Afghanistan, and I had two years professional courses in cinema in Tehran, and participated to many workshops and residency programs in the USA (I was IVLP fellow in 2009), and some other countries. I worked as jury member for numerous national and international film festivals and wrote some article for BBCP, and some other printed and online media networks.
I made several movies about refugees since 1999. I travelled to different countries, documenting the life of refugees while myself was also a refugee. I established Kabul Film Festival and Afghanistan Human Rights Film Festival, and did a lot of other projects. During past 18 years I organized several workshops about human rights, screened more than a thousand human rights movies from all around the glob to Afghan and foreign audiences in Kabul and some other provinces. I also invited many independent filmmakers and human rights activists, mostly from the region, to Kabul to learn and to share their ideas with a broader audiance.
I also worked as senior advisor for National TV and Radio of Afghanistan and as coordinator with some UN projects. In this period of time I build a strong network with human rights activists, mostly with artists, and hold some workshops in some film festivals about dealing with censorship, and security.

Since 2016 I live in Copenhagen.
